Grove Day &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& '''Arthur Grove Day''' (Philadelphia 1904-March 26, 1994, Hawaii) was an author, teacher, and authority on the history of Hawaii, the founding editor in chief of ''Pacific Science: A Quarterly Devoted to the Biological and Physical Sciences of the Pacific Region''. &&&&& &&&&& Day earned his bachelor's and graduate degrees from Stanford University, where he befriended John Steinbeck. He moved to Hawaii in 1944 and was a professor in the English department of the University of Hawaii at Manoa, where he taught a course in "Literature of the Pacific". He chaired the English department from 1948 to 1953.
