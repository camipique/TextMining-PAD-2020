Brinton Collection &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& The '''Brinton Collection''' is a collection of early cinematography that was used by William Franklin Brinton (1857–1919) for his traveling show in the Midwestern United States. It was preserved by history teacher, Michael Zahs, in a barn in Ainsworth, Iowa. The collection includes works by Georges Méliès that were thought to have been lost: ''The Wonderful Rose-Tree'' and ''The Triple-Headed Lady''. The collection's history was recounted in a film documentary, ''Saving Brinton'', in 2017. &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&&
