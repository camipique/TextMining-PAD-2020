Blanca Blanco &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& '''Blanca Blanco''' is an American actress best known for ''The Dog of Christmas, Fake News,'' and ''Torch.'' &&&&& &&&&& Early life and education &&&&& Blanco was born and raised in Watsonville, CA, along with four siblings, by Mexican immigrant parents who worked multiple jobs to support them. She began taking acting and singing classes at a young age, often organizing theatre performances with her siblings, then joined the Gordon Hunt Group and The Groundlings for which she performed in multiple stage productions. She graduated from Washington State University with a Bachelor of Science in Psychology and from Eastern Washington University with a Master of Social Work, in addition to studying Spanish in college. Blanco then worked as a counselor for hospice patients before transitioning to acting. Blanco also trained with Sally Kirkland, John Savage, and Bruce Glover. &&&&& &&&&& Career &&&&& Blanco has appeared in multiple independent films, including ''Showgirls 2: Penny's From Heaven'' in 2011, ''Defending Santa'' in 2013, ''Bullet'' opposite Danny Trejo'','' ''Crimes of the Mind'', ''Anatomy of Deception'', ''Star Trek Equinox: The Night of Time'' and ''Bermuda Tentacles'' in 2014, ''Beverly Hills Christmas'' and ''Dubious Battle'' in 2015, and ''Woman on the Edge'' and the independent thriller film ''Fake News'' in 2017. She also appeared in the films ''Teen Star Academy'' which premiered at Cannes Film Festival in 2016, ''6 Children & 1 Grandfather'' which premiered at Cannes Film Festival in 2017, and ''Mission Possible'' which premiered at Cannes Film Festival in 2018. &&&&& &&&&& In 2016, Blanco was honored with the Arido Award for her contribution to the film industry.
