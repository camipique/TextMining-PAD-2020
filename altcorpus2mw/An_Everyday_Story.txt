An Everyday Story &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& '''''An Everyday Story''''' (German: '''''Eine alltägliche Geschichte''''') is a 1948 drama film directed by Günther Rittau and starring Gustav Fröhlich, Marianne Simson and Karl Schönböck. The film was produced in 1944, towards the end of the Second World War, but was not given a release until DEFA in the Soviet Zone distributed it four years later. It received its Austrian release the following year, and finally in West Germany in 1950. &&&&& &&&&& The film's sets were designed by the art director Otto Hunte and Karl Vollbrecht. &&&&& &&&&& Synopsis &&&&& A novelist completes what he considers to be his masterpiece, but the publisher tells him instead to write an everyday story. &&&&& &&&&& Cast &&&&& * Gustav Fröhlich as Bernd Falkenhagen &&&&& * Marianne Simson as Anneliese Schwarz &&&&& * Karl Schönböck as Herbert Winkler &&&&& * Margot Jahnen as Susi Liebig &&&&& * Hans Brausewetter as Werner, Direktor Exzelsior-Verlag &&&&& * Paul Henckels as Spaziergänger &&&&& * Hans Leibelt as Annelieses Vater &&&&& * Käthe Haack &&&&& * Oscar Sabo as Portier Wudicke &&&&& * Gerda Danker as Sekretärin &&&&& * Armin Schweizer &&&&& * Karl Etlinger as Hausnachbar &&&&& * Hildegard Grethe as Annelieses Mutter &&&&& * Karl Ludwig Schreiber as Gast &&&&& &&&&& See also &&&&& * Überläufer &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& Bibliography &&&&& * Bock, Hans-Michael & Bergfelder, Tim.
