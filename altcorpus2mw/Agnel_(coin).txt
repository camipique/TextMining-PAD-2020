Agnel (coin) &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& An agnel of Philip the Fair &&&&& An '''agnel''', '''aignel''', '''agnel d'or''' or '''mouton dâ€™or''' was a French gold coin, invented by Louis IX of France in the 13th century. The first known examples were struck under Philip the Fair from 26 January 1311 onwards. &&&&& &&&&& The obverse of the coin showed the Paschal Lamb or Agnus Dei, which gave the coin its name. The reverse showed a Gothic cross. The last agnels were struck under Charles VII of France in the 15th century. &&&&& &&&&& Sources &&&&& *http://gallica.bnf.fr/ark:/12148/btv1b7700432x &&&&& &&&&&
