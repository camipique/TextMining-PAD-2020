Baruch Solomon Löwenstein &&&&& Introduction &&&&& '''Baruch Solomon Löwenstein''' (born in mid-nineteenth century, Włodarka, Russia) was a Jewish mathematician. He wrote ''Bikkure ha-Limmudiyyot'', explanations of mathematical passages in the works of Abraham ibn Ezra, Moses Maimonides, and Joseph Delmedigo. He also annotated and published in 1863 a second edition of ''Shebile di-Reḳia'', by Elias ben Ḥayyim Kohen Höchheimer, on the rules of the calendar, with the elements of geometry, trigonometry, and astronomy. &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& * Zeitlin, ''Bibl.
