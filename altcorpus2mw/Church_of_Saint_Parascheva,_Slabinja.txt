Church of Saint Parascheva, Slabinja &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& '''Church of Saint Parascheva''' () is a Serbian Orthodox church located in Slabinja, Sisak-Moslavina County, in central Croatia. It was dedicated to Saint Parascheva of the Balkans. The Church was built in 1828 and demolished during World War II. Only the perimeter walls are preserved. &&&&& &&&&& The Church is located in the center of village Slabinja, on the south side of the D47 road, towards the river Una. &&&&& &&&&& It's under the jurisdiction of the Eparchy of Gornji Karlovac. &&&&& &&&&& History &&&&& The Church was built in 1828 according to the R. Battigelli project from 1819. It was built in baroque building style. &&&&& &&&&& In 1944, during World War II, it's demolished by the Ustashe damaging roof structure, vault, interior and church inventory. After the War ruins remained standing. In 1970 began the reconstruction of this valuable buildings, but roof, unfortunately, was never set up. &&&&& &&&&& On June 4, 2017, Bishop of upper Karlovac Gerasim visited the church. &&&&& &&&&& See also &&&&& &&&&& *Church of the Holy Venerable Mother Parascheva &&&&& * German Opačić &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& External links &&&&& * Official website of Eparchy of Gornji Karlovac &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&&
