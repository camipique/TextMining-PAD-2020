He studied at Queen Mary College in London. &&&&& &&&&& Career &&&&& ===BAC=== &&&&& He joined the Bristol Aeroplane Company in 1957, which became BAC in 1960. Aerodynamic research for Concorde was notably carried out with the BAC 221, which had a droop-nose, and the Dassault Mirage IV. Concorde had an ogee-shaped wing. &&&&& &&&&& ===British Aerospace=== &&&&& With British Aerospace, he became the Chief Engineer of the HOTOL project. &&&&& &&&&& He later became a part-time Professor in Mechanical Engineering and Aeronautics at City, University of London. &&&&& &&&&& Personal life &&&&& He lives in Pucklechurch, a village on the B4465 in South Gloucestershire, close to the M4. He married in 1957.
