Bear Dance &&&&& Introduction &&&&& George Catlin, ''Bear Dance'', hand-colored lithograph, 1844, Buffalo Bill Center of the West. &&&&& '''Bear Dance''' is a Native American ceremonial dance that occurs in the spring. It is a ten-day event to strengthen social ties within the community, encourage courtship, and mark the end of puberty for girls. &&&&& &&&&& For the Utes, it is a ten-day event of dancing, feasting, games, horse racing, and gambling. It is one of the oldest Ute ceremonies. The bear symbolizes leadership, strength, and wisdom.
