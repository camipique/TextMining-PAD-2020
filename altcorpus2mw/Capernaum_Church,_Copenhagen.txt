Capernaum Church, Copenhagen &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& The '''Capernaum Church''' (Danish: Kapernaumskirken ) is a Church of Denmark parish church located at Frederikssundsvej 45 in the Niorth-West district of Copenhagen, Denmark. Inaugurated in 1895, it is the oldest surviving church built by the Copenhagen Church Foundation. &&&&& &&&&& History &&&&& The church was built at the initiative of the Copenahgen Church Foundation (Danish: Kirkefondet). It was built om 1894-05 to design by Valdemar Koch. &&&&& &&&&& Architecture &&&&& The church is built from red brick and has a saddle roof topped by a ridge turret. The facade to Frederikssundsvej has round-arched windows, a Lombard band and a relief of an angel above the main entrance. &&&&& &&&&& Parish &&&&& As of 1 January 2012, the parish had 9,029 inhabitants of which 5,148 (57.02 %) were members of the Church of Denmark. &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& External links &&&&& * Official website &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&&
