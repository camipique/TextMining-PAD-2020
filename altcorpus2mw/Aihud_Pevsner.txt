The family, of Belarusian descent, settled in New York. Pevsner served in the United States Navy from 1944 to 1945, and married Lucille Wolf in 1949. Upon earning a doctorate in physics from Columbia University, he began teaching at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. In 1956, Pevsner joined the Johns Hopkins University faculty. Over the course of his career, Pevsner was received two Guggenheim fellowships, was named a Fulbright Scholar, and granted fellowship by the American Physical Society.. He was the lead researcher credited with the discovery of the Eta meson, and appointed a Jacob L. Hain professor in 1977.
