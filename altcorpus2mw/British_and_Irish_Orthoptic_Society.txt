British and Irish Orthoptic Society &&&&& Introduction &&&&& The '''British and Irish Orthoptic Society''' (BIOS) is a professional body representing orthoptists in the United Kingdom and Ireland. &&&&& &&&&& The society was established in 1937 as the '''British Orthoptic Society'''. By 2002, it had 1,455 members, the large majority of whom were women. &&&&& &&&&& Orthopists in the UK and Ireland are trained at either Glasgow Caledonian University, the University of Liverpool, or the University of Sheffield. Most work for the National Health Service, with a few working for private healthcare organisations, or in the Republic of Ireland. &&&&& &&&&& The society is a member of the Allied Health Professionals Federation and the International Orthoptic Association. It operates a trade union wing, the '''British Orthoptic Society Trade Union''', which is affiliated to the Trades Union Congress. &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& External links &&&&& * Official website &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&&
