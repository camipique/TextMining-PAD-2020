Campbellsville, Tennessee &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& '''Campbellsville''' is an unincorporated community in Giles County, Tennessee, United States. &&&&& &&&&& History &&&&& The settlement is named for Hamilton Crockett Campbell, an early settler. &&&&& &&&&& A post office was established in 1824. By 1860, Campbellsville had a hotel, an Independent Order of Odd Fellows lodge, two general stores, two grocery stores, two churches, two mills, and a population of 150. In 1924, Campbellsville High School opened, and served the community until 1978. &&&&& &&&&& Campbellsville hosts a heritage festival each October. &&&&& &&&&& Notable people &&&&& * Donald Davidson - poet &&&&& * Thomas Hackney - U.S. Representative from Missouri &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& External links &&&&& * Campbellsville Area Association &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&&
