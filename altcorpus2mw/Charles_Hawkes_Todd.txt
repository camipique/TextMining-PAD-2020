Charles Hawkes Todd &&&&& Introduction &&&&& "The College of Surgeons, Dublin". 1837. &&&&& '''Charles Hawkes Todd''' (1784–1826) was the president of the Royal College of Surgeons in Ireland (RCSI) in 1821. &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& Early life &&&&& The son of physician and apothecary Andrew Todd (1750–1795) and Alicia Hawkes (1745–1818), Charles was born in Sligo, Ireland, 6 November 1784. He was educated in a Dublin school, but did not enter the University. Instead, he was indentured to Dr. James Henthorn, who was related by marriage. &&&&& &&&&& In 1804, Charles married Elizabeth Bentley (1786–1862), who was the daughter of Colonel Robert Bentley (1744–1822) and Mary Stanley ( -1820). Colonel Bentley was a member of the East India Company's Service.  &&&&& &&&&& Professional life &&&&& Todd passed his examination on 28 June 1803 at the Royal College of Surgeons in Dublin. In April 1809 he was appointed Surgeon to the House of Industry Hospitals (later became Carmichael School), where he taught anatomy and surgery in a medical school attached to these hospitals In 1819 Todd was appointed Professor of Anatomy and Surgery at the Royal College of Surgeons. In 1821 he became President of the same college. &&&&& &&&&& Personal life &&&&& Charles Todd and Elizabeth Bentley Todd had nine sons and six daughters, and all but one were married. Four of his sons were medical practitioners, three were members of the clergy, one was a barrister, and one was a solicitor. Their first son, James Henthorn Todd, was an Irish scholar and Regius professor of Hebrew at Trinity College in Dublin. Their second son, Robert Bentley Todd, was a London-based physician best known for describing a number of medical conditions, including Todd's palsy. Their youngest son, Armstrong Todd, was also a London-based physician known for his work in anesthesiology and new surgical procedures. &&&&& &&&&& Todd died on 19 March 1826 at No. 3 Kildare-street and was buried at St. James' Church, Dublin. In tribute, the Royal College placed his bust in their principal hall.
