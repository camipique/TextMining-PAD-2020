Banca Rasini &&&&& Introduction &&&&& '''Banca Rasini''' was an Italian bank in Milan, founded in 1950s and acquired by Banca Popolare di Lodi in 1992. The main reason of his fame is that among its customers there was the mafiosi Pippo Calò, Totò Riina, Bernardo Provenzano and Silvio Berlusconi, whose father Luigi Berlusconi was employed in it. The declarations of Michele Sindona about Banca Rasini make it quoted more times by Nick Tosches, a journalist of ''The New York Times'', in his book ''Power On Earth'', and make it famous among the international researchers who are involved about Italian mafia history. &&&&& &&&&& History &&&&& ''Banca Rasini Sas di Rasini, Ressi & C.'' was founded at the beginning of 1950s by Carlo Rasini, Gian Angelo Rasini, Enrico Ressi, Giovanni Locatelli, Angela Maria Rivolta and Giuseppe Azzaretto. The initial capital was 100 millions lire. From its foundation capitals of Lombardy and Palermo flow in it. &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&&
