Asaphodes clarata &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& '''''Asaphodes clarata''''' is a species of moth in the family Geometridae. This species is endemic to New Zealand. &&&&& &&&&& Taxonomy &&&&& This species was described by Francis Walker in 1862 as ''Larentia clarata'' using material collected in Waikouaiti in Otago. George Hudson discussed and illustrated this species under the name ''Xanthorhoe clarata'' in ''New Zealand moths and butterflies (Macro-lepidoptera).'' It has been recorded as being present in Otago and in Canterbury. &&&&& &&&&& Biology and life cycle &&&&& The adults of this species are on the wing in December to February. &&&&& &&&&& Habitat and host species &&&&& ''Asaphodes clarata'' larva &&&&& This species frequents open grassy patches in montane habitat.
