Cleome angustifolia &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& '''''Cleome angustifolia''''', known as '''golden cleome''', '''yellow cleome''' or '''yellow mouse whiskers''', is an African species of plant in the Cleomaceae family. It is common along roadsides and in disturbed areas and is eaten as vegetable locally. Swedish naturalist Peter Forsskål described ''C. angustifolia'' in 1775. It is one of three species in genus ''Cleome'' (the others being ''C. gynandra'' and ''C. oxalidea'') that independently acquired the pathway of carbon fixation.
