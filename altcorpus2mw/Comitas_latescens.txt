Comitas latescens &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& '''''Comitas latescens''''' is an extinct species of sea snails, a marine gastropod mollusc in the family Pseudomelatomidae, the turrids and allies &&&&& &&&&& Description &&&&& The length of the shell attains 21.5 mm, its diameter 10.1 mm. &&&&& &&&&& (Original description) The fusiform shell has an acute spire. The rounded whorls are closely spirally striated. The spire whorls are obliquely plicated. There is a deep, broad groove at the suture. The aperture is ovate. The siphonal canal is rather produced. The body whorl is shorter than the spire. &&&&& &&&&& Distribution &&&&& This extinct marine species is endemic to New Zealand and was found off Mount Brown &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& * Maxwell, P.A.
