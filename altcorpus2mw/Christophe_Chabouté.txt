Christophe Chabouté &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& '''Christophe Chabouté''', (born 8 February 1967) is a French comics artist. &&&&& &&&&& Biography &&&&& Chabouté was born on born February 8, 1967; he is of Alsatian heritage. Chabouté attended Fine Arts courses in Angoulême and Strasbourg. The publishing house Vents d'Ouest published his first pieces in 1993 in Les Récits, a collective album about Arthur Rimbaud. But Chabouté became best known in 1998 by publishing ''Sorcières'' by Éditions du Téméraire (awarded at the Illzach Festival) and ''Quelques jours d'été'' by Editions Paquet (Alph'Art Coup de Coeur Festival Angoulême). He has also illustrated novels for young people. His work ''Tout Seul'' (''Alone)'', which is translated into English by Ivanka Hahnenberger, is considered his masterpiece. &&&&& &&&&& Works &&&&& &&&&& === Albums === &&&&& &&&&& * ''Sorcières'', Le Téméraire, 1998 (rééd.
