He almost immediately obtained a post as Professor of Physics at Madras Christian College and began in this role in the summer of 1877 aged 23. &&&&& &&&&& In 1882 he was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh. His proposers were Alexander Crum Brown, Peter Guthrie Tait, George Chrystal and William Thomson, Lord Kelvin. &&&&& &&&&& In 1891 he left Madras Christian College to become Government Astronomer for Madras, replacing the recently deceased Professor N. R. Pogson. In 1899 he published the New Madras General Catalogue (of Stars) listing 5303 stars visible from the observatory. He left this role in 1899 to become the first Director of the Kodaikanal Solar Observatory where he worked for the rest of his life. &&&&& &&&&& He died at his home in Kodaikanal in India on 27 September 1922.
