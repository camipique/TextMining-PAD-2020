Some sources suggest he was born at Inzievar House rather than Torryburn. Both are close to Dunfermline. &&&&& &&&&& He studied Medicine at Glasgow University gaining his doctorate (MD) in 1856. He joined the British Army in 1857 initially as Assistant Surgeon in the 75th Regiment of Foot and served in India during the Indian Mutiny 1857/8 including being present at the Capture of Lucknow and other events. In October 1859 he moved to be Assistant Surgeon to the 15th Light Dragoons later renamed in 1861 the 15th King's Hussars. The regiment largely remained in India but in 1878 moved to Afghanistan and he saw action on the Second Anglo-Afghan War, there rising to the rank of Surgeon Major. &&&&& &&&&& In 1872 he joined the 78th Highlanders as full Surgeon. In 1879 he sold his Indian Mutiny Medal. &&&&& &&&&& He retired to 26 Buckingham Terrace in Edinburgh near Dean Bridge. He lived there for the rest of his life. &&&&& &&&&& He died in Edinburgh on 9 April 1916. He is buried in the north-west section of Dean Cemetery. &&&&& &&&&& Family &&&&& &&&&& He was married to Elizabeth Mary Beveridge (1839-1912) &&&&& &&&&& They had at least one son: Captain H.
