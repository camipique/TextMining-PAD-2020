Arnold Chaplin &&&&& Introduction &&&&& '''Sir Thomas Hancock Arnold Chaplin''' FRCP (30 August 1864 - 18 October 1944) was a British physician. &&&&& &&&&& After studying at St. Bartholomew's Hospital, Chaplin's primary appointment was at the City of London Hospital for Diseases of the Chest, where he remained for the next twenty-nine years. He co-authored the textbook on ''Fibroid Diseases of the Lung'', and ''The Science and Art of Prescribing'' and wrote ''The Illness and Death of Napoleon Bonaparte'' (1913).
