Carrowcrom Wedge Tomb &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& '''Carrowcrom Wedge Tomb''' is a wedge-shaped gallery grave and National Monument located in County Mayo, Ireland. &&&&& &&&&& Location &&&&& &&&&& Carrowcrom Wedge Tomb is located east-southeast of Ballina village, to the west of Slieve Gamph, in the upper reaches of the Srufaungal River. &&&&& &&&&& History &&&&& &&&&& This wedge tomb was built c. 2500 – 2000 BC, in the Copper or Bronze Age. &&&&& &&&&& Description &&&&& &&&&& Partially renovated. The wedge-shaped gallery is long and wide, orthostats either side. Two roof stones completely cover the gallery. The whole structure is still covered by a U-shaped cairn. &&&&& &&&&& It faces southwest, towards the setting sun. &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&&
