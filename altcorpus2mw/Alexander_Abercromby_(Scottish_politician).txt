By 1703, he married Helen Meldrum, daughter of George Meldrum of Crombie, Marnoch, Banff, minister of Glass, Banff. From 1706 he was an officer in the 21st Foot, the Royal Scots Fusiliers. He was ADC to the Duke of Marlborough in the Low Countries in 1711, and rose to the rank of lieutenant-colonel, retiring on half-pay in 1721. &&&&& &&&&& Abercromby was Commissioner justiciary for the Highlands in 1701 and 1702. He was returned as MP for Banffshire in the Parliament of Scotland in 1706 and after the Act of Union was a representative for Scotland in the first Parliament of Great Britain in 1707. He was returned as Member of Parliament for Banffshire in the British general elections of 1708, 1710, 1713, 1715 and 1722. He did not stand in 1727. He was Lieutenant Governor of Fort William from 1726. &&&&& &&&&& Cope died on 23 December 1728. He and his wife Helen Meldrum had two sons and four daughters.
