Beir Ahmed &&&&& Introduction &&&&& '''Beir Ahmed''' is a prison in Aden, Yemen, operated by the United Arab Emirates. &&&&& &&&&& Operations &&&&& The staff of Beir Ahmed are primarily Yemeni, though the officers are from the United Arab Emirates. Security is provided by Security Belt, a UAE-funded militia. &&&&& &&&&& In July 2018, Yemeni Interior Minister Ahmed al-Maysari demanded that the UAE close or surrender control of the prison. &&&&& &&&&& On July 3, 2018 forty-six detainees were released from the prison. &&&&& &&&&& Abuse &&&&& According to reporting by The Associated Press, detainees at Beir Ahmed have been subjected to torture and sexual abuse. Detainees have been raped, had their genitals electrocuted, and had rocks hung from their testicles. &&&&& &&&&& Detainees at the prison are held without being charged of tried. &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&&
