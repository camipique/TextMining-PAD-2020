Asaphodes citroena &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& '''''Asaphodes citroena''''' is a species of moth in the family Geometridae. This species is endemic to New Zealand. &&&&& &&&&& Taxonomy &&&&& This species was described by Charles E. Clarke in 1934 as ''Xanthorhoe citroena'' using material collected by himself in December 1928 at Waiho Gorge near the Franz Josef glacier in Westland. George Hudson discussed and illustrated this species under the name ''Xanthorhoe citroena'' in his 1939 book ''A supplement to the butterflies and moths of New Zealand''. In 1987 Robin C. Craw proposed assigning this species to the genus ''Asaphodes''. In 1988 John S. Dugdale agreed with this proposal.
