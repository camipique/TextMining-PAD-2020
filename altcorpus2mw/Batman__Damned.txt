Batman: Damned &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& '''''Batman: Damned''''' is a comic book limited series published by DC Comics under its DC Black Label imprint. Written by Brian Azzarello and illustrated by Lee Bermejo, it spans three issues and began on September 19, 2018. Though considered a standalone story, the series is a loose follow-up to the 2008 graphic novel ''Joker''. In ''Damned'', the Joker turns up dead, and Batman recruits John Constantine to help him find the killer. &&&&& &&&&& Publication history &&&&& ''Batman: Damned'' is written by Brian Azzarello and illustrated by Lee Bermejo, with cover art by Bermejo and Jim Lee. It is published by DC Comics as a three-issue limited series and is the first comic of its DC Black Label imprint. It began on September 19, 2018. Azzarello and Bermejo previously collaborated on the 2008 graphic novel ''Joker'', to which ''Damned'' is a spiritual sequel. As it is part of the Black Label line, ''Damned'' will not feature any connections to other Batman stories. Its story will also feature elements of supernatural horror. &&&&& &&&&& The first issue generated a considerable amount of controversy due to a sequence of full frontal nudity in which Batman's penis was exposed. In response, DC announced it would censor future printings of the issue. &&&&& &&&&& Synopsis &&&&& ''Batman: Damned'' is set in a different continuity from the main DC Universe. In the story, the Joker is discovered dead in the water near the Gotham Gate Bridge, and Batman is the prime suspect. Batman enlists the help of John Constantine; together, they delve into the supernatural side of Gotham to find the killer. &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&&
