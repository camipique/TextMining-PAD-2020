Love Without Fear (film) &&&&& Introduction &&&&& '''Liebe ohne Angst''' (''Love without fear'') is a 1989 East German public-education documentary film, directed by Frank Rinnelt. It is 25 minutes long. It was the only HIV/AIDS prevention film made in East Germany. It was produced by DEFA film studios on commission for the German Hygiene Museum in Dresden. &&&&& &&&&& It includes discussion with young people and some erotic scenes. It does not focus on homosexuality. &&&&& &&&&& Synopsis &&&&& The film follows a young filmmaker as she goes to a blood bank to have an AIDS test, while she reflects in a stream-of-consciousness style on her own sex life. She interviews a doctor at the blood bank.
