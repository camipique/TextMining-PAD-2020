Emīlija Gruzīte &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& '''Emīlija Gruzīte''', nee '''Emīlija Upīte''', (7 September 1873, Tirza Parish - March 1945, Riga) was one of the first Latvian women painters at the beginning of the 20th century, attracting public attention from Artists Unit freelancers as well as journalists. &&&&& &&&&& Biography &&&&& Emīlija Upīte was born on 7 September 1873 in the Virāne manor where her father worked. She learned painting with her parish and school teachers. In Riga she became a student of Vilhelms Purvītis on the recommendation of Rūdolfs Blaumanis, and became part of the realist movement. &&&&& &&&&& In 1897, Emīlija married Alfrēds Gruzīte, editor and journalist of the newspapers ''Dienas Lapa, Kuldīgas Vēstnesis'' and ''Kurzemnieks'' &&&&& &&&&& She exhibited her work for the first time in a 1903 group show, and had a solo show in 1907. She continued to exhibit until the First World War. Her poem ''Tevi atstāju'' was set to music by Emīls Dārziņš. &&&&& &&&&& In 2018, her work was shown at the retrospective exhibition ''Âmes sauvages'' in the Musée d'Orsay. &&&&& &&&&& She died in March 1945 in Riga. &&&&& &&&&& Bibliography &&&&& * Red.
