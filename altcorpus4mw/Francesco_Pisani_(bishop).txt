Francesco Pisani (bishop) &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& '''Francesco Pisani''' (died 1572) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Archbishop (Personal Title) of Chioggia (1569–1572) and Archbishop of Naxos (1564–1569). &&&&& &&&&& Biography &&&&& On 15 December 1564, Francesco Pisani was appointed during the papacy of Pope Pius IV as Archbishop of Naxos. &&&&& On 19 July 1569, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Pius V as Archbishop (Personal Title) of Chioggia. &&&&& He served as Bishop of Chioggia until his death on 8 February 1572. &&&&& &&&&& Episcopal succession &&&&& While bishop, he was the principal consecrator of: &&&&& *Miler Magrath, Bishop of Down and Connor (1565); &&&&& *Nicolas Ugrinovich, Bishop of Smederevo (1565); &&&&& *Beatus di Porta, Bishop of Chur (1565); &&&&& *Luca Antonio Resta, Bishop of Castro di Puglia (1565); &&&&& and the principal co-consecrator of: &&&&& *Aloysius Delfino, Bishop of Canea (1565). &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& External links and additional sources &&&&& * (for Chronology of Bishops) &&&&& * (for Chronology of Bishops) &&&&& * (for Chronology of Bishops) &&&&& * (for Chronology of Bishops) &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&&
