Eva Rieger &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& Eva Rieger &&&&& '''Eva Rieger''' (November 21, 1940, Isle of Man) is a German musicologist. After Sophie Drinker, Rieger was the first in the world to work on the social and cultural history of women in music culture. Together with the German-Swiss patron Mariann Steegmann, Rieger developed the idea of a foundation for the advancement of women in music and art, the Mariann-Steegmann-Foundation. In 2012, she was appointed Honorary Senator of the Hochschule für Musik und Theater Hamburg. &&&&& &&&&& Early years and education &&&&& Eva Rieger was a daughter of the German pastor Julius Rieger and the librarian Johanna Krüger Rieger, who was born in London and moved to Berlin in 1953. Eva studied music education, musicology and English language and earned her doctorate in 1976 at the Technical University of Berlin with a thesis on music education in East Germany. &&&&& &&&&& Career &&&&& From 1978-1991, she was an academic councilor at the University of Göttingen and University of Hildesheim; from 1991, professor of historical musicology (focus on the social history of music) at the University of Bremen, and during the period of 1988-1992, Advisory Board member of the journal ''Feministische Studien''. &&&&& &&&&& Rieger gave lectures in the United States, Canada, Japan and Europe. In 1996, she co-founded the section "Women and Gender Research" at the German Society for Music Research. She spoke several times at the "Feminist Theory and Music" conferences in the U.S. In 2000, the Mariann Steegmann Foundation was founded by Mariann Steegmann in collaboration with Eva Rieger. This foundation operates both the "Mariann Steegmann Institute Art & Gender" at the University of Bremen and the "Research Center for Music and Gender" at the Hochschule für Musik, Theater und Medien Hannover. In 2009, she was Corresponding Member of the American Musicological Society. &&&&& &&&&& Research &&&&& Rieger was the first to write a musicological study on the discrimination of women in German music culture as part of the women's movement (women, music and men's rule). Her foci include film music, music education, the life and work of Richard Wagner, as well as gender research in music cultur. Her work not only advocates for the equality of women at all levels of musical culture, but also transferring their analyzes to the music itself. which is not uncontroversial in traditional musicology. She has published several books and numerous articles, including in ''Die Musikforschung'', ''Archiv für Musikwissenschaft'', and ''Feministische Studien''. Her books have been published in Great Britain, Sweden, Japan and South Korea. &&&&& &&&&& Personal life &&&&& Rieger struggled living as a straight woman. She had her first relationship with a woman at the age of thirty, and found salvation with the Homosexuelle Aktion Westberlin (HAW, Gay Action West Berlin) group. &&&&& &&&&& Selected works &&&&&  &&&&& &&&&& * ''Schulmusikerziehung in der DDR''.
