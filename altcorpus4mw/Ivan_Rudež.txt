Ivan Rudež &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& '''Ivan Rudež''' (born 22 December 1979) is a Croatian basketball coach who currently coaches PS Karlsruhe Lions of the German ProA. &&&&& &&&&& Career &&&&& Rudež started as a coach at KK Zrinjevac in 2004, where he coached both U18- and senior teams. In 2007, he went to Slovakia, where he coached BC Prievidza until 2010. After that, Rudež was named assistant coach at Croatian club KK Cedevita. In the summer of 2011, he took over the head coaching position at BC Vienna in the Austrian Bundesliga, he parted ways with the club in late November 2011 after compiling a record of 3 wins and six losses in the Austrian top flight and then moved KK Cibona, where he served as assistant to head coach Jasmin Repeša. He signed as head coach with Swiss Lions de Genève, where he worked from 2012 to 2016. He was named coach of the year twice, won two national championships and three national cup titles. From 2016 to 2018, Rudež coached the Slovakia national basketball team. &&&&& &&&&& In November 2018, Rudež accepted the position as head coach of the PS Karlsruhe Lions in the German second-tier ProA league.  &&&&& &&&&& His younger brother, Damjan Rudež, is a Croatian professional basketball player, who played for the Indiana Pacers, Minnesota Timberwolves and Orlando Magic in the NBA. &&&&& &&&&& ==References== &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&&
