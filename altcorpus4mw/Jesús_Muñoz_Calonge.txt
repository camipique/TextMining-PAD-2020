Jesús Muñoz Calonge &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& '''Jesús Muñoz Calonge''' (born 1 January 1976) is a Spanish retired footballer who played as a central midfielder, and is the current assistant manager of UD Almería. &&&&& &&&&& Playing career &&&&& Born in Mota del Cuervo, Cuenca, Castile-La Mancha, Muñoz was an Albacete Balompié youth graduate. On 2 September 1995, aged just 19, he made his first team – and La Liga – debut, starting in a 0–3 away loss against Sporting de Gijón. &&&&& &&&&& Muñoz was a first-choice option during the 1995–96 season, which ended in relegation. He continued to feature regularly for ''Alba'' in the following campaigns, scoring his first professional goal on 10 November 1996 in a 3–1 Segunda División away defeat of Atlético Madrid B. &&&&& &&&&& On 10 July 2001, Muñoz was presented at Atlético Madrid, freshly relegated to the second division. He contributed with only 12 appearances, achieving top tier promotion as champions. &&&&& &&&&& In 2002, Muñoz signed for Deportivo de La Coruña and was immediately loaned to Real Zaragoza in the second division. On 31 August 2004, he moved to fellow league team Terrassa FC also in a temporary deal. &&&&& &&&&& Returning to ''Dépor'' in 2005, Muñoz only featured in one league match (16 minutes in a 2–1 away win against CA Osasuna on 26 February 2006) before leaving the club. He subsequently represented RSD Alcalá and CP Villarrobledo, retiring with the latter in 2008 at the age of 32. &&&&& &&&&& Managerial career &&&&& In 2012, Muñoz was appointed assistant of Paco Jémez at Rayo Vallecano. He followed Jémez at Granada CF, but after the manager's appointment at Cruz Azul, both ended their partnership. &&&&& &&&&& On 7 December 2017, Muñoz was announced as Chen Jingang's assistant at Changchun Yatai F.C. of the Chinese Super League.
