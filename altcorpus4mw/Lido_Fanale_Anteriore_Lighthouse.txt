Lido Fanale Anteriore Lighthouse &&&&& Introduction &&&&&   &&&&& &&&&&   &&&&& &&&&& '''Lido Fanale Anteriore Lighthouse''' () is an active lighthouse located on the northern tip of the island of Lido di Venezia, in the Venetian Lagoon on the Adriatic Sea; the place where is the main access to Venice by ship route. &&&&& &&&&& Description &&&&& The lighthouse, established in 1912, consists of a quadrangular skeletal tower, high, covered, not wholly, by a metal plate with balcony and light. The tower is settled on a platform supported by wooden piles; the covering is painted with black and white horizontal bands. &&&&& &&&&& The '''Fanale Anteriore light''' is positioned at above sea level and emits one white flash in a 3 seconds period, visible up to a distance of . The lighthouse, completely automated and powered by a solar unit, is managed by the Marina Militare with the identification code number 4177 E.F. &&&&& &&&&& The '''Fanale Anteriore Direction light''' is positioned at above sea level and emits one green flash in a 4 seconds period, visible up to a distance of .
