Hill's Store &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& '''Hill's Store''' was a commercial building located on Grand Marais Avenue in Grand Marais, Michigan> It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1973, but demolished and de-listed that same year. &&&&& &&&&& In the 1890s, Wilkes W. Hargrave and Roy C. Hill were operating a store in Seney, Michigan. When that town began dying, in about 1895, they moved to Grand Marais and opened a new store, called "Hargrave and Hill". By the 1920, the store was called just "Hill's Store." The store remained open until 1951. &&&&& &&&&& The building was a two-story frame structure on a cement foundation. It had a false front and a cornice. &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& 		 &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&&
