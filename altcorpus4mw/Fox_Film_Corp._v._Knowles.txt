Fox Film Corp. v. Knowles &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& '''''Fox Film Corp. v. Knowles''''', 261 U.S. 326 (1923), was a United States Supreme Court case in which the Court held the statute intends that an executor, there being no widow, widower, or child, shall have the same right to renew a copyright for a second term as his testator might have exercised had he continued to survive. &&&&& &&&&& This case was reaffirmed in ''Miller Music Corp. v.
