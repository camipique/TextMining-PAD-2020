Empire I: World Builders &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& '''''Empire I: World Builders''''' is a 1981 video game published by Edu-Ware Services Inc. It is the first game in the ''Empire'' trilogy, followed by ''Empire II: Interstellar Sharks'' (1982) and ''Empire III: Armageddon'' (1983). &&&&& &&&&& Gameplay &&&&& &&&&& Set during the initial colonization period of a galactic Imperial civilization, the player chooses one of three classes (miner, missionary, or homesteader) and departs the New York city spaceport to practice their chosen trade on the newly colonized planets. &&&&& &&&&& Reception &&&&& Rudy Kraft reviewed ''Empire I: World Builders'' in ''The Space Gamer'' No. 51. Kraft commented that "I cannot recommend buying the game. The general system is interesting, and another game with fewer flaws would be a top-notch product, but ''World Builders'' is not." &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& External links &&&&& * &&&&& * ''Empire I: World Builders'' review at ''The CRPG Addict'' &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&&
