Hiroyuki Kanno (game designer) &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& '''Hiroyuki Kanno''' (菅野 ひろゆき, 8 May 1968 - 19 December 2011) was a Japanese video game designer who wrote and directed erotic adventure games and visual novels starting in the 1990s. Some of his most well-known games include ''Xenon'', ''Desire'', ''Eve Burst Error'' (at C's Ware) and ''YU-NO'' (at ELF Corporation), which had a major influence on the genre. He was friends with Ryu Umemoto, who often worked closely with him and composed music for the games. In December 1997, he founded Abel corporation and became its CEO.
