Jamie Paik &&&&& Introduction &&&&& '''Jamie Paik''' is a Professor and Director of the Reconfigurable Robotics Lab at École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne. &&&&& &&&&& Early life and education &&&&& Paik was born in Canada. Her father was a scientist and her mother was a painter. She completed her Bachelor's degree at University of British Columbia in 2002. She majored in mechanical engineering. After graduating, she spent a year working at Mitsubishi Motors in Tokyo. She earned her PhD at Seoul National University, sponsored by Samsung Electronics. Her PhD considered the limbs of anthropomorphic robots. &&&&& &&&&& Research &&&&& Paik worked as a postdoctoral assistant in Pierre and Marie Curie University, where she worked on laparoscopic tools. JAIMY, a 5 mm size motorised robot, can be used to assist suturing during surgery. It is available commercially through Endocontrol Medical. She joined Harvard University as a postdoctoral assistant, working with Erik Demaine, Daniela L. Rus and Robert Wood. &&&&& &&&&& In 2012 Paik founded the Reconfigurable Robotics Lab at École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne. She is an active member of the Swiss National Center of Competence in Research. Her recent work focusses on robotic origami, ''robogami''. She developed vacuum-powered Soft Pneumatic Actuators for compliant robotics. She was an invited speaker at the Obama Admininstration's Women Inspire Innovation workshop in 2013. She was a judge at the 2016 and 2018 Soft Robotics Toolkit competition.
