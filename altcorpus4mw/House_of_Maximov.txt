House of Maximov &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& The '''House of Maximov''' () is a building in Rostov-on-Don built in the middle of the 19th century in style classicism. It is one of the most aged stone constructions of the city which remained up to now. The merchant P. R. Maximov was the first host. Maximov's house has the status of an object of cultural heritage of regional value. &&&&& &&&&& History and description &&&&& In 1867 at Staraya (Bazarnoy) square of Rostov-on-Don the two-storeyed stone profitable house was built. It belonged to the dealer by grain P. R. Maximov owning ssypka and mills. According to some assumptions, Troph√Æme Charginsqui was the author of the project of the building. In 1868 in Maximov's house the new room of public places was open, and the City Council, merchant and petty-bourgeois managements passed there. The City Council sat at Maximov's house till 1899 when for it the new building on Bolshaya Sadovaya Street was built. &&&&& &&&&& Besides City Council in Maximov's house also other institutions were at different times marked: women's school of M. L. Levkova, office of the merchant head, school of factory tutoring, sports school, design institutes. In Maximov's house there was also a set of merchant benches. &&&&& &&&&& In the 2000s (decade) the building replaced several owners, various shops and offices were placed there. During the same period disputes on the security status of the house of Maximov began. In April, 2006 the Leninsky district court of Rostov-on-Don deprived Maximov's house of the status of an object of cultural heritage. Further the prosecutor's office tried to appeal against this decision. Maximov's house underwent reconstruction and re-plannings. In 2006 on the building the commemorative plaque was established, but in 2011 during repair of a facade it was removed. Later the new board was established. &&&&& &&&&& The building is located on crossing of Stanislavsky Street and Semashko Lane. The main facade is turned to cathedral of the Nativity of the Theotokos. The building is built in traditions of late classicism. Window openings of the ground and second floor have semi-circular completions. The eaves with an arkaturny belt finish a decor of facades. The central part of the main facade is emphasized with the mezzanine with a number of small arch windows. &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&&
