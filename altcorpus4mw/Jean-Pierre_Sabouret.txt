Jean-Pierre Sabouret &&&&& Introduction &&&&& '''Jean-Pierre Sabouret''' (4 December 1944 – 25 April 2007) was a French classical violinist. &&&&& &&&&& Career &&&&& Winner of several international awards ( in 1963, Golden Medal of the Maria Canals International Music Competition of Barcelone in 1964), Sabouret was invited by many orchestras in France and abroad. A member of prestigious chamber music ensembles including the Loewenguth Quartet, he later joined the Via Nova Quartet. &&&&& &&&&& A soloist of the Contemporary classical music "Ensemble l'Itinéraire" for ten years, then of the Orchestre philharmonique de Strasbourg (1974-1976), he joined the orchestra of the Paris Opera in 1977, where he became violin solo. He held these positions until 2006. &&&&& &&&&& A pedagogue, he founded a music school in Ablon-sur-Seine in the Paris region. He was also professor of violin and chamber music at the Conservatory of Athis-Mons (Essonne), then at the Paul-Dukas Conservatory (12th arrondissement of Paris).
