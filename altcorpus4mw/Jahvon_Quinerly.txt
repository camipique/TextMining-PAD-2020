Jahvon Quinerly &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& '''Jahvon Quinerly''' (born November 25, 1998) is an American college basketball player who formerly was a highly-regarded recruit for the Villanova Wildcats of the Big East Conference. He attended Hudson Catholic Regional High School in Jersey City, New Jersey and was one of the top-ranked players in the nation as a senior. &&&&& &&&&& High school career &&&&& On August 8, 2017, Quinerly committed to join the University of Arizona, but he reopened his recruitment in October after federal documents suggested that he had taken a $15,000 bribe from the team's assistant coach Emanuel Richardson, who had been arrested during the 2017–18 NCAA basketball corruption scandal. On February 14, 2018, despite strong recruitment efforts from Oklahoma University, he committed to Villanova University.. &&&&& &&&&& In his final two seasons for Hudson Catholic Regional High School, Quinerly earned back-to-back Gatorade New Jersey Boys Basketball Player of the Year honors. He was named to the West roster for the 2018 McDonald's All-American Boys Game, playing against high school teammate Louis King. He finished with 3 points and 4 assists, shooting 1-of-7 from the field. &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& College career &&&&& ===Villanova=== &&&&& Quinerly joined a Villanova team fresh off a National Championship and needing to fill a roster who lost 4 starters. Many expected him to play a role similar to Jalen Brunson who was 5 star recruit and started as a freshman, but the minutes never really seem to come for Quinerly. After a loss to Penn Quinerly took to instagram with a story that read, “Was my second choice for a reason”. Quinerly was committed to Arizona before an FBI investigation led him to decommit and sign with Villanova. &&&&& &&&&& On April 3, 2019, Quinerly decided to transfer from Villanova after appearing in 25 of 36 games during his freshman season &&&&& &&&&& Personal life &&&&& Quinerly is a member of the basketball collective "Jelly Fam," centered around flashy finger roll layups, which he helped create with Minnesota basketball guard Isaiah Washington. &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& External links &&&&& * Villanova Wildcats bio &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&&
