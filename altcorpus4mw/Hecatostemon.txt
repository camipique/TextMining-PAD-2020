111	Hecatostemon
Hecatostemon &&&&& Introduction &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& '''''Hecatostemon completus''''' is a species of shrub or tree native to northeastern South America and is the only member of the genus '''''Hecatostemon'''''. &&&&& &&&&& Taxonomy &&&&& Formerly classified in the Flacourtiaceae, phylogenetic analyses based on DNA data indicate that this species, along with its close relatives in ''Casearia'', ''Samyda'', ''Laetia'', and ''Zuelania'', are better placed in a broadly circumscribed Salicaceae. &&&&& &&&&& Description &&&&& ''Hecatostemon'' differs from its close relatives in having numerous stamens in three series and one ring of staminodes, or "disk," inside the stamens. &&&&& &&&&& Distribution and habitat &&&&& The species is found in tropical deciduous forests, matorrales, savannahs, and even saline flats in northern Brazil, Colombia, Guyana, Peru, and Venezuela. &&&&& &&&&& References &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&& &&&&&
