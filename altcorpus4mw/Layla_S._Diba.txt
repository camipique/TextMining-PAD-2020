Diba, in memory of Mahmood T. Diba, to Metropolitan Art Museum &&&&& '''Layla Soudavar-Diba''' (Persian: لیلا سودآور-دیبا) is a scholar of 18th/19th-century and contemporary Persian art. She is also curator of various exhibitions in the field such as the 1998-1999 exhibition Royal Persian Paintings: The Qajar Epoch 1783-1925 at the Brooklyn Museum. &&&&& &&&&& Biography &&&&& &&&&& ===Negarestan Museum=== &&&&& Layla Diba was the founding director of Negarestan Museum (Persian: موزه نگارستان) in Tehran from 1975 to 1979. The museum was shut down during the 1979 Iranian Revolution. The Negarestan Museum was established by Queen Farah Pahlavi to promote the Persian art of the 18th and 19th-century. &&&&& &&&&& ===Other activities=== &&&&& Layla Diba has been an art advisor of various organisations such the Metropolitan Museum of Arts. She currently lives in New York and is a member of Encyclopædia Iranica's board of trustees and the Soudavar Memorial Foundation. &&&&& &&&&& ===Personal life=== &&&&& Layla Diba was born in a Persian-American family. She married the businessman Mahmoud T. Diba who was among the victims of Swissair Flight 111 crash in 1998. Diba has a son Ahmad-Reza. &&&&& &&&&& ===Education=== &&&&& Layla S.
